dear sir,

re-character assassination as a political weapon

may i say that i sympathise with both correspondents who approve of character assassination as a political weapon and with those who do not. whether these stories are not the past is the past and should not be resurrected we should judge public officers according to their present public image and prsent public action, not with references to their past private lives as it they have no right to privacy at all

may i point out, however,that it is a very dangerous weapon? for even though i appprove of character assassination, i am also aware of the risks involved in using it.for example, if you use it against someone who is able to disapprove your allegations you will only have destroyed your own credibility and i will have raised the reputations of your opponent. it seems that few politicians say to themselves,"i had better not that story about him in case he publishes this story about me!" indeed, i find it very surprising that many politicians employ this weapon as if there are no dangerous to themselves in using it

however,i share the view already expressed that it is most unfair to seek to damage your rival reputations by referring to unwise acts commited many years ago which have not been repeated since that time, it has been correctly pointed out that accusations like that can be truthfully made against almost anyone.

so in conclusion may i say that,even though i agree that it is a legiti mate political weapon,i think it is one that ought to be used very sparingly if a candidate convices us that he is going to carry out the policies of our party, then he will have deserved our support when he stands for election

yours faithfully,
Temitope
